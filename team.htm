Our Team

Meet the Team

Ridley Graduate Boat Club is supported by a small but dedicated team of individuals who help keep the club running smoothly year-round. This group includes experienced coaches, committed board members, and passionate volunteers who give their time and energy to ensure every aspect of the club—from daily operations to long-term planning—is handled with care. Their efforts make it possible for our athletes to focus on training and competing, knowing they have a strong support system behind them.


These individuals are more than just administrators or helpers—they're stewards of our culture and champions of the sport. Their commitment to the club's values, community, and future is what allows RGBC to grow sustainably. They help build the kind of environment where athletes of all ages feel welcomed, supported, and motivated. Whether it's coaching on the water, organizing events, or quietly working behind the scenes, this group plays a vital role in shaping the RGBC experience.

Sean Leyland

Head Coach & Boathouse Manager

Sean has been the Head Coach for RGBC for the past 3 summers. This summer he's also taken on the role of Boathouse Manager. His dedication to the sport and our athletes has been instrumental in maintaining the high standards of excellence that RGBC is known for.

Eric Woelfl

Olympic Athlete & Coach

Many years rowing internationally for Canada, culminating in the 2016 Rio Olympics. Eric brings his wealth of experience and competitive spirit to help develop the next generation of rowers at RGBC. His dedication to the sport and his role as a devoted dad inspires all who work with him.

Penny Smith

Masters Women's Squad

Penny is a member of our Masters Women's Squad and brings incredible strength and determination to the team. Her commitment to the sport and her teammates exemplifies the spirit of RGBC. She continues to inspire athletes of all ages with her dedication and passion for rowing.

Melanie Teini

Board Member & Masters Athlete

Mel, on the right, grew up in Niagara and recently returned. She rowed for Michigan for 4 years. Part of the RGBC Masters Women's squad and has been on the RGBC board for 2 years. Mel is a force to be reckoned with, bringing both competitive experience and administrative expertise to our club.
